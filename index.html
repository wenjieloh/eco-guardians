Protect Our Ecosystem!

Invasive plants are "aliens" in our backyard. Learn how to spot them and stop them.

What are Invasive Species?

An invasive plant isn't just a weed—it's a species brought from another part of the world that grows out of control because it has no natural enemies here.

Where Do They Hide?

Invasive species are tough. They often thrive in disturbed ground, like:

  • Roadsides: Where seeds drop from car tires.
  • Riverbanks: Where water carries plant fragments to new soil.
  • Backyards: Where "pretty" garden plants escape into the wild.

What To Do: The Citizen Science Guide

🚫

Don't Move Firewood

Invasive bugs and seeds travel in wood. Buy it where you burn it!

👟

Scrub Your Boots

Seeds hide in the mud on your shoes. Brush them off after a hike.

📱

Report It!

Use apps like iNaturalist to snap a photo and alert scientists.

Quick Summary:

  1. Report It: Use apps to log the location.
  2. Clean Your Gear: Don't carry seeds from one park to another.
  3. Native Only: Plant local flowers, not "aliens."
